# Video Script

## Title
The Secret Behind Recommendation Systems: How Netflix, Spotify, and TikTok Recommend What You Like

## Layousa's Part

Hi everyone. Our project is about recommendation systems and how apps like Netflix, Spotify, and TikTok recommend content we may like.

This topic is interesting because most of us use these apps every day. Sometimes it feels like the app knows exactly what we want to watch or listen to next. But behind the screen, software is using data, algorithms, and machine learning.

A recommendation system is software that suggests content to users. It tries to predict what someone may enjoy based on their actions.

For example, if someone watches many cooking videos, TikTok may show more cooking videos. If someone skips a song quickly, Spotify may learn that the user does not like that type of song. If someone watches many mystery shows, Netflix may recommend more mystery or crime shows.

This topic is related to software development because developers build these systems. They design how data is collected, how patterns are found, and how recommendations are shown to users.

Recommendation systems use algorithms, machine learning, user behavior, and user experience design. This shows that software development is not only about coding. It is also about understanding what users need and how apps can feel more personal.

## Zarlasht's Part

Now we will explain how Netflix, Spotify, and TikTok use recommendation systems.

Netflix recommends movies and shows based on what users watch, search for, rate, and what similar users enjoy. For example, if a user watches many romance or crime shows, Netflix may suggest similar shows or movies.

Spotify recommends music based on listening habits. It can learn from songs users play, skip, save, search, or add to playlists. For example, if someone saves many songs by the same artist, Spotify may recommend more music from that artist.

TikTok recommends videos on the For You page. It learns from likes, comments, follows, videos watched, videos watched until the end, and videos marked as not interested.

Recommendation systems have many benefits. They save time, help users discover new content, and make apps feel more personal. They also improve user experience because users can find movies, songs, and videos they may enjoy.

However, there are also concerns. Apps may collect too much personal data. Users may spend too much time scrolling. People may also only see content similar to what they already like, which can create filter bubbles.

In conclusion, recommendation systems are powerful software tools. They shape what we watch, listen to, and enjoy every day.

Our opinion is that recommendation systems are useful, but they should be designed responsibly. Users should understand how their data is being used, and companies should not only use recommendations to keep people addicted to apps.
