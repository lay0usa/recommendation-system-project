# Slides Outline

## Slide 1: Title
The Secret Behind Recommendation Systems

How Netflix, Spotify, and TikTok Recommend What You Like

Team Members:
- Layousa Farahi
- Zarlasht Najimzada

## Slide 2: Introduction
Recommendation systems are software tools that suggest content users may like.

Examples:
- Netflix recommends movies and shows
- Spotify recommends songs and playlists
- TikTok recommends videos on the For You page

## Slide 3: How They Work
Recommendation systems collect information from user actions.

They may look at:
- What users watch
- What users listen to
- What users like
- What users skip
- What users search
- How long users spend on content

## Slide 4: Netflix Example
Netflix recommends movies and shows based on user activity.

Examples:
- What users watch
- What users search for
- What users rate
- What similar users enjoy

## Slide 5: Spotify Example
Spotify recommends music based on listening habits.

Examples:
- Songs users play
- Songs users skip
- Songs users save
- Searches
- Playlists

## Slide 6: TikTok Example
TikTok recommends videos on the For You page.

Examples:
- Likes
- Comments
- Follows
- Videos watched until the end
- Videos marked as not interested

## Slide 7: Benefits
Recommendation systems can:
- Save time
- Help users discover new content
- Make apps feel personal
- Improve user experience

## Slide 8: Problems and Concerns
Recommendation systems can also create problems:
- Privacy concerns
- Too much data collection
- Addictive scrolling
- Filter bubbles

## Slide 9: Our Opinion
We think recommendation systems are useful, but they should be designed responsibly.

Users should understand how their data is being used.

## Slide 10: Conclusion
Recommendation systems are powerful software tools.

They shape what we watch, listen to, and enjoy every day.
